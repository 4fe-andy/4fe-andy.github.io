States of matter

In a solid, the particles are

  • arranged regularly
  • only able to vibreate about fixed position

In a liquid, the particles are still

  • mostly touching
  • but arranged randomly

in a gas, the particles are still

  • arranged randomly
  • but move randomly

The temperature at which the solid melts si called its melting point. The particles in the liquid have more kinetic energy than the particles in the solid so energy has to be supplied to convert a solid to a liquid.



The temperature at which the liquid freezes is called the freezing point.

HINT: 沸腾与蒸发在考试中一般可以混用，但是两者略有区别。
Boiling and evaporation are generally used in the exam, but there is a slight difference between them.

  • 蒸发指在任何温度下都能进行的，一般发生在液体表面的汽化现象。
    Evaporation refers to the phenomenon of vaporization that can be carried out at any temperature and generally occurs on the surface of a liquid.
  • 沸腾指在一定压强下，液体温度升高到一定程度时，液面和液体内部同时发生迅速汽化，涌现出大量气泡的现象。
    Boiling refers to the phenomenon that under a certain pressure, when the liquid temperature rises to a certain extent, the liquid surface and the liquid interior simultaneously occur rapid vaporization, and a large number of bubbles emerge.

Diffusion

The spreading out of particles in a gas or liquid is known as diffusion.

Particles of different gases travel at diferent speed

The white ring of ammonium chloride takes time to form (as it takes some time for the particles of ammonia and hydrogen chloride to diffuse along the tube), and appears closer ot the hydrochloric acid end.

Explain why it appears closer ot the hydrochloric acid end

Because, ammonia particles are lighter than hydrogen chloride particles and therefore move faster.

Explain why the white ring of ammonium chloride takes time to form

Because

  • Gas particles move in random direction
  • Gas particles collide with air/other particles
  • Gas particles collide with the walls

Diffusion in liquid

Explain why diffusion through a liquid is very slow if the liquid is comletely still

Because

  • The particles in a liquid move slowly than the particles in a gas
  • The particles in a liquid are much closer together, thus, there is less space for particles to move.

The dilution of coloured solutions

The Solubility of Solids

Solutes, solvents and solutions

When a solid dissolves in a liquid:

  • the substence that dissolves is called the solute
  • the liquid it dissolves in is called the solvent
  • the liquid formed is a solution.

A saturated solution is a solution which contains as
much dissolved solid as possible at a particular temperature.

Measuring solubility

The solubility is the mass of solute which must dissolve in 100g of solvent at that temperature ot form a saturated solution.
