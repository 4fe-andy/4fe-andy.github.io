Visual Model Based Question Bank Retrieval
2024-09-03 18:26:52 # Projects #Projects #Coding #Algorithm

Introduction

I often deepen my understanding of exam questions by directly learning the standard answers after truly grasping the underlying logic of the knowledge. I consider this method an effective supplement to classroom learning, especially for explanatory and descriptive question types. This method helps me quickly establish the correct thought process and word usage for answering questions. It is quite similar to the learning model in deep learning—given an input and an output, the model calculates the error between the output and the correct answer, and then updates the model through backpropagation until the error is minimized. Without timely comparison with the correct answers, the brain cannot form effective positive feedback (backpropagation). Of course, one can only develop generalization ability by truly understanding the internal logic of the knowledge; otherwise, it leads to overfitting.

However, searching for answers online after completing a question is often troublesome, and sometimes I even forget to look them up. After finishing a whole set of papers, the thought process I had while solving earlier questions often becomes vague, lacking the quick and clear positive feedback needed for self-correction. Therefore, I came up with an idea: could I quickly identify the content of the questions by taking a photo and then retrieve the corresponding answers? This is how the idea for this project was born.

Strategy

I plan to use Apple’s machine learning framework to recognize text in images, then use Sentence Embeddings to calculate the similarity between the recognized text and each question stored in a JSON file. Finally, the question with the highest similarity will be selected, and the corresponding answer will be provided.

Implementation

Required Frameworks

  • SwiftUI/UIKit for building the software’s interactive interface
  • Vision for recognizing text in images
  • NaturalLanguage for implementing Sentence Embeddings to determine the similarity between sentences

引言

在学习过程中，我会在真正理解了其中的知识逻辑之后，通过直接学习标准答案的方式来加深对考题的理解。我将这种方法视为课堂学习的一种有效补充，尤其在解释和描述题型上，这种方法能够帮助我迅速建立起答题的正确思路和用词习惯。这与深度学习中计算机的学习模式颇为相似——给定输入和输出，计算输出与正确答案之间的误差，然后通过反向传播更新模型，直至误差最小化。如果不能及时和正确答案对比，大脑就无法形成有效的正反馈（反向传播）。当然，只有真正理解了知识内在的逻辑，才能形成泛化能力，否则会过拟合。

然而，写完题目后再去网上寻找答案往往非常麻烦，有时甚至会忘记去查找。等到完成一整套试卷后，自己当时如何思考前面题目的过程也常常模糊不清，缺乏快速而清晰的正反馈来帮助自我修正。因此，我产生了一个想法：能否通过拍照来快速识别题目内容并检索到对应的答案？于是搭建这个项目的想法应运而生。

思路

使用Apple提供的机器学习框架识别图片中的文字，然后使用 Sentence Embeddings 方法计算出和存储在JSON文件中的每一个题目的相似度，最后选择相似度最高的题目，输出题目相应的答案。
